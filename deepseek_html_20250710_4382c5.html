Key Differentiators

  • ETFs combine stock-like trading flexibility with the diversification benefits of mutual funds, making them ideal for cost-conscious investors
  • Bonds provide predictable income streams and capital preservation but typically require larger initial investments
  • Unit Trusts offer professional active management at higher costs, suitable for investors who prefer a hands-off approach
  • ETFs have significant advantages in trading flexibility, cost efficiency, and tax treatment compared to other options
  • Each investment type serves different objectives: Bonds for stability, ETFs for efficient market exposure, and Unit Trusts for active management strategies
